Response Guide

Lead Incident Checklist

1 Safety and Containment Immediate Response

  • ▹
    "Is everyone safe?" (Safety is always the first priority).
  • ▹
    "What specifically happened to cause the stop?" (Defect, machine breakdown, material shortage, or safety issue).
  • ▹
    "Is the problem contained?" (Ensuring defective parts do not move to the next station).
  • ▹
    "What is the symptom of the issue?" (Fixing the symptom to get the line moving). [1, 2]

2 Diagnosis and Root Cause 5 Whys Analysis

  • ▹
    "Why did this happen?" (Asking "Why" multiple times to find the root cause, not just the symptom).
  • ▹
    "Was the machine running to standard?" (Checking if the operator was following standardized work).
  • ▹
    "What changed?" (Was it new materials, a change in personnel, or a tool change?)
  • ▹
    "Did the yellow light happen first?" (Checking if the issue was ignored or if it escalated too quickly). [1, 2, 3]

3 Immediate Action And Future Prevention

  • ▹
    "How can we get the line running again safely?" (Focusing on a quick, safe restart).
  • ▹
    "Do we need maintenance or engineering support?"
  • ▹
    "What is our plan to prevent this from happening again?" (Updating work standards or fixing equipment to ensure the issue does not recur).
  • ▹
    "Do we need to update our standard operating procedures (SOPs) based on this?" [1, 2, 3]
